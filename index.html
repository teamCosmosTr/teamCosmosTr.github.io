Introduction

Eutrophication is a global environmental issue resulting in degraded water quality, changes in ecosystems and economic distribution in industries like agriculture, fishery, and tourism. Our Kraken is a cutting-edge water filtration system that uses a cellulose-graphene filter membrane to improve water quality and fight eutrophication.
The purpose of Kraken is twofold: to provide an eco-friendly alternative to traditional water filtration systems and to repurpose invasive algae that harm aquatic ecosystems. With our easy-to-use, modular filtration units, consumers can integrate Kraken into water treatment facilities, agricultural irrigation systems, and industrial processes.

Why Kraken?

Chemical treatments, mechanical filtration systems, and biological remediation methods are the conventional treatment means our innovation competes with. These approaches may work well for some classes of pollutants, but due to their high operational cost (i.e., power driven procedures), the need for power and the contaminant scavenging capabilities it possesses, these are usually limited. Despite that, our cellulose-graphene membrane technology offers a complete solution by effectively managing inorganic and organic contaminants.
Kraken provides a new strategy for stopping water pollution and eutrophication. Unlike the usual filtration systems which often use artificial materials and a lot of energy, our algae-based technology is economical, sustainable, and ecologically benign.

Impact on the Environment and Society:

Kraken uses invasive algae in order to restore aquatic ecosystems by reducing the negative effects that their proliferation causes and reestablishing the equilibrium of impacted water bodies. Our efforts have also produced readily available clean water. Our filtering technology supports public health and economic development by ensuring that communities, businesses, and agricultural systems have access to clean water.
Reducing Waste: By converting algal waste, Kraken transforms the environmental hazard into a useful resource.
